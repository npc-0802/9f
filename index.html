Small choices inside a data center — what filters to load, how air moves, which setpoints to hold — drive equipment health, energy use, climate damages, public-health damages, and operating economics. 9 Foundations quantifies those consequences and helps operators reduce them.

01 · Why it matters

Data centers are a high-stakes building type.

The healthy-buildings framework — that the air inside a space drives the health and performance of what happens in it — applies to server halls as forcefully as it does to offices and schools. The economics just look different.

Watch the air. Score the building.

Real-time building data closes the gap between modeled performance and actual conditions, giving operators a low-cost way to verify air-quality and building-health outcomes in the field. Every metric matters twice — once for the equipment that needs stable operating conditions, once for the people in and around the building.
