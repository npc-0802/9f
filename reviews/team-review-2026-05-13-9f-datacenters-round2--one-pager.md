## What the review says, in one page

Round 2 satisfied the definitive execution brief. The three credibility-undermining interactions Round 1 flagged — the cosmetic FilterStudio A/B toggle, the MATRIX heatmap that didn't teach, and the random-on-every-toggle H.E.A.A.L. dashboard — have all been rebuilt with honest, source-driven behavior. The $39.9M finding has been promoted out of the five-up pillar grid into a hero-tier banded result with the four CoBE breakdowns attached as captioned children. Tabular numerics, gradient discipline, hover semantics, section rhythm, and scroll-spy all landed. The team section was reframed from "bios coming soon" placeholder into an intentional "Disciplines on the engagement" capability narrative. Every primary interactive surface now confirms the page's analytical claims instead of betraying them. All four reviewers say the page is now safe to ship and safe to put into outbound — and three of them moved their trust scores up by 2–3 points.

The remaining residue divides cleanly into three categories. *Small things the implementation could close inside a half-sprint:* UI's four `fs__headline-stats` supporting tiles are still tile-shaped and read as competing with the headline rather than captioning it; UX caught a small markup-vs-state divergence in `.hl__status` (the hardcoded `<li>`s get rewritten by `updateStatus` on load — works, but fragile); UX also caught the 7d/30d range chips on H.E.A.A.L. as the last piece of inert theater (the JS comment literally says "the chip just records user intent"); and the page uses both `~$40M` and `~$39.9M` for what should be the same number. *One in-scope BD/buyer-experience push the implementation didn't take:* BD and Apollo both flag that the primary CTA is still mailto when a static Calendly link would qualify as "static-friendly" under the brief's no-backend constraint and would convert materially better — neither of them say the page is broken without it, but both say the next conversation-shortening change is the calendar link. *One thing the brief explicitly left out and Apollo still flags as the ceiling:* one anonymized customer engagement, even abstract enough to avoid naming a client — that's not a Round 2 implementation problem, it's a Round 3 asset-supply problem.

UI puts the bar plainly: the page made a confident half-tier jump and the remaining craft work is "token-and-selector size, not redesign." UX puts the trust shift plainly: the page used to lose credibility within five seconds of poking and now actively reinforces it. Apollo puts the buyer shift plainly: "Round 1 I would have forwarded this to my sustainability lead; Round 2 I would mention it to my COO." BD puts the BD shift plainly: "Adoption call: I'd link to this in cold outbound tomorrow and feel materially better about it than v1."

## Net assessment

Round 2 is a clear ship within the brief's intentional scope. The implementation made the disciplined, brief-respectful call to fix what was actually broken (interactive theater, hierarchy, team placeholder language) instead of chasing what was out of scope (productized SKUs, fabricated case studies, pricing pages). The single most important truth from this review: the page now passes the only test that mattered after Round 1 — every interaction on the page is at least as credible as the static copy around it, and several are now meaningfully *more* credible because they let the reader inspect the science instead of being asked to take it on faith. Where Round 1 said "do not let the dashboards undercut the science," Round 2 said "fixed" — and the four reviewers agree.

## What next sprint should focus on

- **Swap the primary CTA's mailto for a static Calendly link.** A single `href` change — no backend, no CRM, no scope creep. BD names this as the single highest-leverage remaining BD gap; Apollo agrees it's in-scope; UX flags the mailto friction. The brief's no-backend prohibition was aimed at multi-step funnels, not at third-party calendar links. Capture warm prospects before they tab away.
- **Demote the `.fs__headline-stats` supporting tiles into a caption strip.** Drop the tile chrome (backgrounds, borders), bring `b` from 1.55rem to ~1.2rem, gap them 28px with a top border. The `$39.9M` becomes 3× louder by subtraction. UI's headline craft find.
- **Eliminate the last interactive theater on the page.** Either wire the H.E.A.A.L. 7d/30d range chips to swap sparkline data, or remove them. Strip the hardcoded `.hl__status` `<li>`s so the list renders entirely from JS. Drive the Society pillar from the same `FS_VALUES` data model as the other three pillars (or label it explicitly as scenario-invariant). Three small fixes; collectively they close the last "is this real?" doubt.
- **Add the static-only conversion architecture pieces the brief left room for.** Pre-select a flagship site in the inventory panel on load (Ashburn campus or equivalent) so the side panel demonstrates capability instead of showing "Hover or tap a site." Add a single in-section CTA inside the inventory ("Want your portfolio modeled? Request the inventory brief →"). Add a one-line geographic intent statement ("Virginia today; the same parameterization works for any market"). All in-scope, all under an hour each.
- **Reconcile `~$40M` (hero) with `~$39.9M` (FilterStudio).** Use one number consistently. Trivial copy fix; eliminates a small inconsistency a careful reader would notice.
- **Document — outside this page — the one thing the brief correctly excluded but that caps the page's ceiling: an anonymized engagement.** Apollo's clear next-deliverable problem. Not a Round 3 implementation ask; a Q2 asset ask. Frame this as the next thing 9F should ship *to* this page, not the next thing the implementation should ship *on* this page.
