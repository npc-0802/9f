## ux_agency_lead — UX (flow / friction / motion)

### 1. Overall verdict

Round 3 is the round where the page stops feeling like a coordinated visual system and starts feeling like an instrument. The branding pass — real logo, hero scale-rule SVG replacing ambient particles, restrained card chrome, type-led hierarchy, brand left-rule on the $39.9M banner — finally gives the page a consistent register that matches the high-tech / building-science intent of the brief. Most of Round 2's open issues have been closed honestly: the Society pillar is now explicitly framed as "scenario-invariant · across all 412 sites," the dead H.E.A.A.L. range chips are gone, the MATRIX axes are correctly oriented with BETTER/WORSE direction markers, and the four inventory histograms are wired through the same `applyFilters → renderHistograms` calculation path as the map and legend (app.js:291–323). What still falls short is exactly the spine of the north-star story: the map-to-archetype "zoom-in" the brief described literally doesn't exist, the archetype is a hardcoded block that doesn't reconcile with the site the user just hovered on the map, and the archetype particle motion that's supposed to dramatize "more outside, fewer inside" is still the silent-fade-at-the-filter pattern I flagged in Round 2. The page is mature, on-brand, and credible — the three products land. The map → archetype handoff is the one place the north-star is still being told *around*, not told.

### 2. Top strengths

- **The inventory histograms are a real addition, not chart-junk.** The four strips below the map (`hist-floor`, `hist-it`, `hist-pue`, `hist-filters`, index.html:204–219) render an active-subset bar against a faint ghost outline of the full 412-site distribution (app.js:342–387), through the same single calculation path that drives the legend counts and the "Showing N of N sites" caption (app.js:291–323). Toggling Existing / Planned / DLC / DEC visibly reshapes each strip in unison. This is exactly the brief's framing — "the variation across them is the point" (index.html:158) — finally executed mechanically. A reader now *sees* that there is no generic Virginia data center.
- **The $39.9M moment carries the conviction the brief asked for.** The hero-tier banner (index.html:378–394) reads as one decision compounded across the portfolio: the 6rem teal-cyan gradient number, the "Statewide finding · 412 modeled sites" / "Methodology · Harvard CoBE" eyebrow pair (index.html:380–382), the editorial "A single filter decision, applied across 412 sites…" body copy, and the four breakdowns (`206,419 MWh`, `$36.7M`, `$2.83M`, `$369k`) attached as captioned children with tabular figures. The 2px brand left-rule (styles.css:754) anchors it visually as a 9F finding, not a generic data card. This is the page's loudest moment, correctly.
- **MATRIX teaches the surface in one read.** The axis ticks are now correct (low U on the left, high HVAC at the top, app.js:1026–1042), the "← BETTER" / "WORSE →" / "BETTER ↑" direction markers (app.js:1044–1046) remove all ambiguity about gradient direction, and the white-ringed best cell with a dashed leader line to a callout that says `Best regime · X kWh/m² · −2.0% vs baseline` (app.js:1121–1138) answers "so what" without the reader having to do mental math. The four scenarios genuinely *reshape* the field via per-scenario `uWeight/hWeight/offset` (app.js:982–987), not just shade it. This is the most credible interactive surface on the page.
- **The hero's static scale-rule SVG is the right move.** The technical hairline rule with major / minor ticks (index.html:48–75) reads as instrument-language — measurement, building science, calibration — without firing any of the "generic AI shimmer" tells the ambient particles used to. Combined with the restrained 56px grid mask and the brand logo, the hero now lands the audience in the register the brief asked for (high-tech, 9F-aesthetic) inside the first breath.
- **The H.E.A.A.L. floor toggle is fully deterministic and the section is honest.** Each `FLOORS["1"|"2"|"3"]` profile in app.js:1181–1221 hardcodes score, alerts, status note, tile values, and a 4×18 severity pattern string. Toggling Fl 1 / Fl 2 / Fl 3 reproduces the exact same building state every time — no `Math.random` anywhere — and the dead 7d/30d range chips from Round 2 are gone. The section now does exactly what it claims.

### 3. Top issues

- **The brief's "scroll → zoom into a data center → archetype" sequence isn't built.** The brief is explicit: *"when you scroll it'd like zoom into one of the data centers and show something like the data center archetype we have."* What ships is two independent sections — `#inventory` and `#archetype` — separated by section padding and a section-divider hairline (styles.css:115). There is no zoom, no transition, no shared state. The archetype panel (index.html:262–287) hardcodes "Total building volume 743,224 m³ / Total IT capacity 280 MW / Outdoor PM₂.₅ 6.49 µg/m³" in static HTML; clicking or hovering a site on the map updates `#site-kv` but never touches `#arch-svg` or `.arch__panel`. The page's whole "single calculation path" discipline — the rule that every interactive readout reconciles with active state — gets honored on the histograms and gets dropped at precisely the moment the brief asked for it to be loudest. *Why it matters:* the north-star is a narrative arc from 412-sites-of-variation → "here is one of them, look how it actually breathes" → "here are the products that act on that mechanism." Right now the page ships 412 → generic archetype → products. The reader gets the variation argument and then the archetype argument; they don't get the handoff.
- **The archetype particle motion still isn't didactic.** Same state machine I flagged in Round 2 (app.js:586–664): 85% of intake particles fade silently inside the INTAKE rectangle (`state: "fading"`, app.js:603–606), 15% become cyan interior, 60% of those fade at exhaust, the remainder exit. The math is correct; the staging hides the moment of comprehension. With 110 particles drifting at any time and silent fade-out as the dominant "filtration" cue, the visual reads as "amber particles approach the building and ambient sparkle happens" — not as "look how few make it past the filter." There is no spatial moment at the filter face, no visible flux differential between INTAKE and EXHAUST counted-per-second, no dust accumulation. A first-time reader who skips the panel copy walks away with motion, not with the filtration argument. Given that this section is the brief's only *visual* embodiment of "buildings have an inside and an outside and that matters," it's still the lowest-credibility interactive surface on the page.
- **The first 9–10 seconds don't quite frame the public-health / climate / economics quadruple.** The hero headline ("Healthy building decisions have *high-stakes* consequences in data centers") and the sub copy mention equipment health, energy, climate damages, public-health damages, and operating economics in a single paragraph (index.html:81–85). That's a lot to absorb at landing speed. The hero proof strip immediately below presents *10% / $39.9M / 412* — three numbers, all good — but the three north-star themes (equipment + energy / public health / climate, all with economic implications) aren't surfaced as a structured triad anywhere above the fold. The Why section ("Data centers are a high-stakes building type") does the lifting via four stat cards, but a reader who only scans the hero gets the brand and the numbers without the *story shape*. The brief led with "the choices you make in buildings matter… equipment health, energy, public health, climate — all with economic implications" — that exact frame should be the spine of the hero or the immediate first-scroll, not a deferred reveal in section 01.
- **The static `.hl__status` `<li>` items in the HTML still duplicate JS-rendered state.** Index.html doesn't actually ship hardcoded `<li>`s anymore (I checked — the `.hl__status` ul is empty at index.html:496); the JS at app.js:1262–1271 writes them on first paint. So this is now closed, but only because the markup-side was stripped — verifying that no future edit reintroduces the duplication is worth a re-check. **(Crediting Round 2; not a new issue.)**
- **The map → site-detail panel uses lowercase abbreviation `DLC` / `DEC` in the filter chips with no glossary anywhere on the page.** The filter chips at index.html:194–196 say `DLC` and `DEC` and the site-detail panel renders `System type: Direct liquid cooling (DLC)` (app.js:441) — but the chip labels themselves are unexpanded and a reader who hits the section cold doesn't know what DEC stands for (Direct Evaporative Cooling, presumably). For a page that's pitching to a mix of building scientists *and* infra / COO buyers, the buyer half loses a beat trying to decode the chip group. Either spell them on first use ("Direct Liquid · Direct Evap") or annotate via a tiny mono helper line below the chip row. The brief explicitly listed "HVAC system type" as one of the variables the inventory carries — the page should let a layperson read that variable.
- **The hero number `10%` and the Why section's `10%` stat card are presenting the same finding twice with slightly different phrasing.** Hero proof: "of US electricity projected for data centers by 2030" (index.html:93). Why card: "Projected share of total US electricity use by data centers by 2030" (index.html:125). It's the same fact, two cards apart, with the same number. Not wrong — but the second one isn't earning its place; the slot would be more valuable carrying a different load-bearing stat (the 9F-proprietary one, e.g., "412 sites modeled" or the air-fraction range). Right now Why#1 echoes Hero#1.

### 4. Highest-leverage recommendations

- **Build the map → archetype handoff as a single shared selection.** When the user clicks (or focuses) a site on the VA map, the archetype section should populate from that site's data — building volume from `floor_m2 × ceiling_m`, IT capacity from `it_mw`, system type from `system`, air fraction from `air_frac`, filter count from `filters`, outdoor PM from the site's `egrid` region. Add a "View this site as an archetype →" affordance on the map's site-detail panel that smooth-scrolls to `#archetype` and updates its panel in flight. The archetype's "Representative NV Archetype" framing can stay as a fallback when nothing is selected; the moment a real site is chosen, the archetype reflects it. This is one calculation-path extension and it closes the largest north-star gap on the page.
- **Add a single didactic moment to the archetype particle viz.** Either (a) collapse the 85%-filtered population to a thin compressed band at the INTAKE face that visibly *accumulates* over a 20-second dwell, then resets — making "this is the filter, particles stop here" spatially explicit — or (b) render a permanent counter on each side of the building (`OUTDOOR FLUX 3.2/s` left, `INDOOR FLUX 0.5/s` right) that updates live from the particle population. Either gives the section a hook a first-time reader can narrate. The state machine math is already correct; the staging just needs one visible moment.
- **Pull the four-theme north-star frame above the fold.** Either restructure the hero proof strip into a four-tile triad ("Equipment · Energy · Public health · Climate" with `→ all with economic implications` as the connector copy) or move that framing into a single 2-line eyebrow under the headline. Currently the hero proof is doing numbers; the brief asked it to do *story shape* first.
- **Spell the HVAC abbreviations on first use.** Change the filter chips from `DLC` / `DEC` to `Direct Liquid` / `Direct Evap` (or pair the chip label with a one-line mono helper directly below the filter row explaining what the toggle does). The four-letter codes belong in the dense site-detail panel where the rest of the spec language lives.
- **Replace the duplicated `10%` Why card with a 9F-proprietary stat.** Slot 04 in Why already carries "5 outcomes 9F quantifies" — slot 01 doubling the hero's data center share number costs the page a load-bearing position. Substitute "412 modeled sites" or the inventory's air-fraction / IT-load range, so the Why grid carries four *distinct* arguments.

### 5. What I would watch next

- After the map-archetype handoff: can a first-time reader narrate the page as "412 sites, all different → here's one of them, look at how it breathes → here are the tools that act on that"? If the archetype still feels like a generic stock illustration after handoff, the handoff isn't earning its keep.
- After the particle re-stage: drop the archetype's right-hand panel temporarily and watch a colleague narrate just the SVG. If they say "particles outside, particles inside, fewer inside" — it lands. If they say "particles drift toward the building" — it doesn't yet.
- On a 360px phone: the hero proof strip and the histograms both collapse to single-column. Watch whether the four breakdowns under the $39.9M banner still let the headline number breathe, or whether they stack into a long mono-typed run that costs the hero its emphasis.
- After the next round, watch how the inventory histograms behave when the user filter-stacks (e.g., Existing + DLC). The active bars should noticeably shrink — that's the variation argument made physical. If they don't, the histograms aren't communicating subset-vs-whole legibly enough.

### 6. Scorecard

- **Usability: 8.5/10** — every interactive surface now does what it advertises and the histograms are a real upgrade, not a flourish. Half a point off for the missing map → archetype handoff and the abbreviation issue; the rest is solid.
- **Clarity: 8/10** — the spine of the page reads cleanly section-to-section, the $39.9M finding is unambiguously the headline, and MATRIX teaches its surface in one breath. A point and a half held back: the four-theme north-star frame isn't surfaced above the fold, and the archetype's particle viz still doesn't make filtration legible to a non-reader.
- **Trust: 9/10** — Round 2's open trust issues are all closed (Society pillar honestly labeled, dead H.E.A.A.L. chips removed, MATRIX axes corrected, $39.9M numbers reconcile to the breakdowns). One point held back for the archetype-vs-map discontinuity: the page advertises "9F translates inventory parameters into realistic per-site archetypes" (index.html:258) and then ships an archetype that doesn't change when the user picks a site. A careful reader will catch that gap.

The H.E.A.A.L. pips especially: three glowing dots in a small status list is exactly the "generic dashboard SaaS" tell the brief was trying to leave behind.
- **Ambient radial gradients survived in two high-visibility containers.** `.map-canvas` still ships `radial-gradient(800px 400px at 25% 40%, rgba(56,189,248,0.06)...)` (styles.css:520–526), and `.closing__bg` still ships `radial-gradient(800px 380px at 80% 70%, rgba(94,234,212,0.08)...)` (styles.css:1058–1062). The brief says the ambient radial-gradient backgrounds were cut. - **Pillar vs. tile metric vocabulary disagrees.** Inside FilterStudio, `.pillar__metric b` is `display: block` so the unit `<span class="u">` drops to the next line (styles.css:704–706). Inside H.E.A.A.L., `.tile__val span` sits inline to the right of `<b>` with `margin-left: 6px` (styles.css:922). These are the same atom — "metric value + unit" — composed two different ways.
