## ux_agency_lead — UX (flow / friction / motion)

### 1. Overall verdict

Round 2 is a real correction, not a polish pass. The three interactive surfaces I called out last round — FilterStudio's cosmetic toggle, the MATRIX heatmap that didn't teach, and the H.E.A.A.L. grid that re-rolled randomness on every floor click — have all been rebuilt with honest, source-driven behavior. The $39.9M finding has been promoted to a hero-tier banner. Scroll-spy and the progress rail are present and tasteful. The page now reads less like a sales-deck-in-HTML and more like a credible analytical surface. Residual issues are smaller and almost all sit in the archetype motion and a handful of section-language inconsistencies — not in the core interactions anymore.

### 2. Top strengths

- **FilterStudio toggle is now honest.** `setFsMode` in app.js:922–928 redraws both charts, swaps the primary pillar values (`fs-energy-a/b`, `fs-health-a/b`, `fs-perf-a/b` via `updatePillars` at app.js:894–921), and rewrites the `#fs-viewing` copy. A/B/Compare now means something — Compare is the default, single-filter mode dims the other curve and bolds the active one (`strokeWidthFor`/`opacityFor`, app.js:688–694), and the pillars get a subtle accent border via `pillar--A-active` / `pillar--B-active` (styles.css:673–676). This is the single biggest credibility recovery on the page.
- **MATRIX is a decision surface now, not a colored grid.** Per-scenario `uWeight/hWeight/offset` values in `SCENARIOS` (app.js:964–969) actually reshape the gradient — envelope retrofit shifts dominance to HVAC, setpoint tune amplifies envelope sensitivity, "All combined" lowers the floor. The color-scale legend strip (`lower energy use → higher`, app.js:1038–1040), a white ring on the best cell with a leader-line callout (app.js:1080–1115), and a delta-vs-baseline label (`−X.X% vs baseline`) all answer "so what" without the reader having to do mental work.
- **H.E.A.A.L. ships three deterministic profiles.** `FLOORS` in app.js:1158–1198 hardcodes per-floor scores (82/86/91), alert counts, status notes, tile values, and a 4×18 severity pattern string. Toggling floors now reproduces the same building state every time. The `paintFloor` + `updateScore` + `updateStatus` + `updateTiles` pipeline (app.js:1216–1278) is exactly the kind of fixed profile the brief asks for — no `Math.random` anywhere in the section.
- **$39.9M is now a hero-tier moment, not one of five equal tiles.** The `fs__headline` block (index.html:341–359; styles.css:704–743) sits below the pillars/charts as a full-width banded result with the four breakdowns (`206,419 MWh`, `$36.7M`, `$2.83M`, `$369k`) attached as captioned children. The pillar grid was simultaneously demoted to 4-up (Energy/Health/Performance/Society), which keeps the headline number from competing with itself.
- **Tabular numerics are wired across the whole metric family.** styles.css:79–88 applies `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` + `tnum`/`lnum` features to `.proof__num`, `.stat__num`, `.counter`, `.pillar__metric b`, `.hl__score-num span`, `.card--imp b`, `.fs__headline-num`, `.fs__headline-stats b`, `.tile__val b`, `.mx__readout-row b`, `.kv dd`, `.map-ranges span`, `.legend__chip b`, and every `[data-countup]`. Digits no longer wobble during count-up — a half-tier of perceived craft for one rule block.
- **Scroll-spy + progress rail land cleanly.** `IntersectionObserver` in app.js:63–95 picks the top-most visible section and sets `is-active` on the matching topnav link; the active-state underline (styles.css:284–290) plus a thin cyan `topnav__progress` bar scaled by scroll percentage (app.js:48–57, styles.css:292–301) gives the long page real orientation without becoming chrome. Reduced-motion is honored — `transition-duration: 0.001ms` collapses the rail's easing, and the bar still tracks position.
- **Card hover bloom is correctly scoped.** styles.css:245–246 restricts the cyan border glow to `.card--interactive`. Static stat/impact/role cards no longer pretend to be clickable.

### 3. Top issues

- **The archetype particle flow still doesn't make filtration legible.** The state machine in app.js:568–646 is the right idea — outside → intake → 85% filtered, 15% interior → exhaust — but the visual result is still subtle. With 110 particles at any one time and a fade-out (`state: "fading"`, app.js:629–638) rather than a visible collapse at the filter face, the dominant impression is a stream of amber dots drifting toward the building and quietly disappearing. There is no spatial moment of "this is the filter, particles stop here," no funnel into the INTAKE bay, no visible difference in *flux* between outdoor and indoor. The very few cyan particles inside the small interior rectangle and the rare exhaust-side particle (40% of an already-15% subset) read as "isolated indoor sparkle" rather than "fewer made it through." A reader who skips the panel copy still sees ambient motion, not didactic motion. *Why it matters:* this is now the lowest-credibility interactive surface on the page; everything else (FilterStudio, MATRIX, H.E.A.A.L.) was upgraded to teach, and the archetype lags behind that bar.
- **The Society pillar still reads as a hand-wave compared to the other three.** Energy/Health/Performance pillars now have real A-vs-B mechanics from `FS_VALUES` (app.js:684–687) and update with the toggle. The Society pillar (index.html:304–311) is hardcoded to `$3.2M climate + health avoided · portfolio-scale · Modeled co-benefits` and never changes regardless of toggle state. After the upgrade to the other three, this pillar visibly breaks the pattern — it's the one tile where switching A/B/Compare does nothing. Either drive it from the same data model (e.g., Filter A's society co-benefits vs Filter B's) or label it explicitly as "scenario-invariant · statewide rollup" so the user knows why it doesn't move.
- **The hero proof number `~$40M` and the FilterStudio headline `~$39.9M` are inconsistent against each other.** Both refer to the same statewide finding (`~$40M` in the hero, app.js renders `data-countup="39.9"` in the FS headline, and the closing CoBE breakdown sums to $39.9M). The hero is a rounded approximation while the FS section gives the precise value with a `~` qualifier. For a careful reader scrolling top-to-bottom, the two numbers don't quite line up — small but the brief explicitly flagged the approved public claims (`~$39.9M` / `~$40M`) and asked for them to be presented at the precision they were approved at. Trivial copy fix, but worth catching now.
- **H.E.A.A.L.'s static-status block in the markup contradicts the live status block.** index.html:464–468 hardcodes `Actions clear · 2 alerts (PM₂.₅, RH) · 0 limits exceeded` directly in the HTML, but `updateStatus` (app.js:1239–1248) re-renders `.hl__status` from the FLOORS profile on first paint. The default Floor 2 happens to match (`2 alerts (PM₂.₅ · RH)`), so this works on load, but the markup-vs-state duplication is fragile — a reader who arrives with JS still loading sees one copy, the JS then rewrites it, and during the transition the labels flicker between "PM₂.₅, RH" and "PM₂.₅ · RH" (comma vs middle dot). Drive the status list entirely from JS or strip the hardcoded `<li>`s.
- **The 7d/30d range chips on H.E.A.A.L. are dead.** app.js:1320–1324 explicitly acknowledges this — "Range toggle — no-op for now (data is the same regardless of range); the chip just records user intent." That's the Round 1 anti-pattern in miniature: a visible control that does nothing under interaction. After the rest of H.E.A.A.L. was rebuilt to be honest, leaving these chips as no-op cosmetic affordances reintroduces exactly the trust hit the brief was trying to close. Either give the sparkline tiles a 7d-vs-30d data series, or remove the chips. Currently they're the only piece of theater left in the section.
- **The closing CTA's secondary mailto loads body context but lands the user inside a mail client they may not have configured.** index.html:642 — the `Request the inventory brief` button is a `mailto:` with a preloaded subject and body. The brief explicitly allowed this as a static-friendly secondary path, and it's better than nothing, but on a fresh browser (no default mail app, mobile webview, corporate environment) clicking it produces either silent failure or a system-modal "choose mail app" dialog. The button has no `aria-describedby` or visible note explaining "opens your email client." For a page targeting infra/COO buyers — half of whom live in webmail — at least pair it with the email address as a visible fallback in the button's hover/label, or add a small line of secondary text below explaining the click contract.

### 4. Highest-leverage recommendations

- **Make filtration visible at the filter face.** Replace the silent fade of the 85% filtered population with a moment of compression at the INTAKE rectangle — collapse particles to a thin band of dust accumulation on the filter face, hold for a frame, then fade. Either that or render a permanent "loaded with X g" indicator on the front of the INTAKE bay that grows as the section dwells. The current state machine's *math* is correct; the *staging* hides the moment of comprehension.
- **Drive the Society pillar from the same data model.** Add `society` to `FS_VALUES` (app.js:684–687) — e.g., `A: { society: 3.2 }`, `B: { society: 2.1 }` — and update `updatePillars` to swap a real comparison. Or replace the pillar with an explicit "Portfolio rollup · scenario-invariant" framing so the reader understands why it doesn't change. Either ends the asymmetry; the current state half-implements the toggle's contract.
- **Either wire 7d/30d to real data or remove the chips.** Easiest honest path: give each tile two sparkline series, swap by `data-hl-range`, change tile thresholds/values where appropriate. Or: delete the chips entirely. Don't ship a comment in production that says "the chip just records user intent."
- **Strip the hardcoded `.hl__status` `<li>`s.** Render the entire list from `updateStatus` on first paint. Tiny change; closes a markup-vs-state divergence that a careful reviewer will catch.
- **Reconcile `~$40M` (hero) with `~$39.9M` (FilterStudio).** Use one number consistently. The cleanest move: keep the hero at `~$40M` only if the FS headline is `~$40M` too, or move both to `$39.9M` with a `~` qualifier. The methodology footnote can absorb the precision.

### 5. What I would watch next

- After the archetype rework: does a first-time reader, *without reading the panel copy*, walk away with "outdoor particles → filtered → fewer indoor"? If not, the motion still isn't didactic. Drop the panel temporarily and watch how someone narrates the SVG.
- After the Society pillar fix: does the FilterStudio panel feel symmetric again — all four pillars reacting to the toggle as a family? Right now the Society pillar is a visible "this one doesn't move" tell.
- On a phone at 360px wide: the FS headline grid (`fs__headline-grid` collapses to single column at 900px and stats go 1-up at 480px, styles.css:769–773). Watch whether the four breakdown stats stack legibly under the `$39.9M` hero, or whether the section becomes a long scroll of monosized numbers losing the hero's emphasis.
- Whether the scroll-spy active state survives fast scrolling. The IO config (`threshold: [0, 0.15, 0.5, 1]`, `rootMargin: "-80px 0px -50% 0px"`) is reasonable, but with eight sections and a long page, fast-flicking the trackpad on macOS sometimes leaves the active state lagging the scroll-position. Watch for stuck-active states during real-user scrubbing.

### 6. Scorecard

- **Usability: 8/10** — every primary interaction now does what it advertises. FilterStudio toggle, MATRIX scenarios, H.E.A.A.L. floor toggle: all honest. Half a point off for the dead 7d/30d chips and the still-subtle archetype motion; the rest of the page is solid.
- **Clarity: 8/10** — section spine still wayfinds well, the $39.9M hero-tier banner finally lets the headline finding read as the headline finding, and the MATRIX best-cell callout teaches the surface in one read. The Society pillar's frozen-while-everything-else-moves behavior costs half a point; the inconsistent `~$40M` vs `~$39.9M` costs another half.
- **Trust: 8/10** — randomized H.E.A.A.L. is gone, MATRIX scenarios actually reshape the field, FilterStudio toggle drives both charts and numerics, the CoBE methodology is named in the headline finding's eyebrow. Two points held back: the no-op range chips reintroduce the smallest version of the Round 1 anti-pattern, and the markup-vs-state divergence in `.hl__status` is the kind of fragility a careful reader notices. Substantially recovered from the 5/10 Round 1 mark.

### 3. Top issues

- **`.fs__headline-stats b` reads as four competing tiles, not as captioned children of the `$39.9M`.** Each supporting stat sits at `font-size: 1.55rem` in `--ink-1` (styles.css:755–762) inside its own bordered card with `padding: 16px 18px` and `background: rgba(0,0,0,0.28)` (styles.css:748–754). Next to a `clamp(3.8rem, 8vw, 6.2rem)` headline that's at most ~2.3× louder, not the 2–3× the brief asked for — and worse, the four tiles together carry more visual mass than the single number because they tile a wider column. The fix is to demote them: drop `b` to `1.15–1.25rem`, switch tile background to `transparent` or `rgba(255,255,255,0.015)`, lose the border (the panel already has one), and let them read as a caption strip under the headline rather than a 2×2 grid that wants equal share of voice. System-level inside this banner.
- **The "M" suffix on the count-up lives inside the gradient text and prints in proportional weight.** `.fs__headline-num` (styles.css:731–741) is one inline-flex container, gradient-clipped — fine — but the actual rendered string at end-of-count is `"39.9M"` from `data-countup="39.9" data-suffix="M"` (index.html:348, app.js:125). That "M" comes out the same display-weight as the digits, eating the visual rhythm that `.fs__headline-stats .u` (styles.css:763) gets explicitly with `font-size: 0.76em`. The headline number deserves the same unit-demotion treatment the supporting stats already have — split the `M` into a `<span class="u">` like the hero proof block does (index.html:71) so it sits at ~0.4–0.5em and the digits dominate. Consolidate: either make `.pillar__metric span` inherit from `.u`, or wrap unit text in `<span class="u">` everywhere and delete the metric-span rule. One-off but pattern-wide across pillars.
