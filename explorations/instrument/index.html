01 Field brief 9 Foundations · Data Centers

Healthy building decisions have high-stakes consequences in data centers.

Small choices inside a data center — which filters to load, how air moves, which setpoints to hold — cascade through equipment health, energy use, climate damages, public-health damages, and operating economics. 9 Foundations quantifies those consequences and helps operators reduce them through low-touch modeling and field monitoring.

Methodology · Harvard CoBE

Energy deltas, translated to human and climate cost.

9 Foundations uses Harvard's Co-Benefits of the Environment (CoBE) tool to translate modeled changes in electricity use into estimated public-health and climate damages avoided. That converts a percentage point of savings into the language operators, regulators, and communities all share — dollars, lives, and emissions.
